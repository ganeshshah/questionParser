Q.): What is the key benefit of a fixed interest rate loan?
1.It allows for flexibility in EMI payments.
2.It offers lower interest rates over time.
3.It provides security and stability during economic changes.
Options:
(a) 2 Only
(b) 4 Only
(c) 3 only
(d) 1, 2 and 4
(e) 2 and 3 only
Solution: (C)

Explanation: Fixed interest rates offer both the borrower and the lender with stability and assurance. Depending on the economic condition, the variable interest rate may decline or rise in the near future. If the interest rate rises, the borrower's EMI or tenor rises, which is bad for the borrower, but if the interest rate falls, the borrower's EMI or tenor falls, which is bad for the bank. As a result, a fixed interest rate provides stability and security to both parties.

Q.): What does the term "Incremental CRR (I-CRR)" refer to?
1.The minimum percentage of liquid cash a bank must maintain as per RBI regulations.
2.The RBI's decision to withdraw ₹2,000 notes from circulation.
3.An additional cash reserve requirement is applied to deposits made after a specific date.
Options:
(a) 1 Only
(b) 2 Only
(c) 3 and 2 only
(d) 1, and 3 only
(e) 3 only
Solution: (B)
Explanation: I-CRR occurs when banks experience an unexpected deposit excess. As a result, banks would increase their lending, inject more money into the economy, and boost inflation. To guarantee that extra liquidity is absorbed by the RBI in the form of Incremental or additional CRR, a separate CRR is applied to deposits made as of a specific date.
